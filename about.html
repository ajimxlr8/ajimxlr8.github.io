Chemical Engineer,
Researcher & Leader

I am an undergraduate student in Chemical Engineering at Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), Dhaka — one of South Asia's most competitive engineering institutions. I maintain a CGPA of 3.78/4.00, placing me on the Dean's List for two consecutive academic years and earning merit scholarships for four semesters.

My research interests span two exciting frontiers: CRISPR-based diagnostic platforms for point-of-care testing, and functional materials for environmental remediation. I currently contribute to a literature review on CRISPR/Cas systems at the POCT Research Group, while earlier work at ABRI focused on synthesising cellulose oxalate sorbents from waste newspaper to tackle antibiotic pollution in wastewater.

Beyond the laboratory, I am deeply committed to building scientific communities. As Regional Chair of the AIChE Southern Asia Subcommittee, I lead a network of over 50 student chapters — coordinating events, mentoring liaisons, and fostering collaboration among young engineers across the region.
